<ReportSection number="01" title="Bulk Transfer Widget — Key Numbers" description="Data covers Jan 1 – Aug 31, 2024. A significant product change on April 11 renamed the widget from 'Transfer' to 'Bulk Transfer' and added an extra click to access it. The numbers before and after that date tell very different stories.">

<Callout title="April 11 change:">

The widget label changed from *"Transfer"* to *"Bulk Transfer"* and moved behind an extra click. Daily interactions dropped by **~95%** — from ~1,004 to ~47 per day. This is not a failure: it filters out users who were accidentally landing on a bulk flow they didn't need.

</Callout>

<Callout title="The signal behind the noise:">

Post-change, users who do reach the widget are *much more intentional* — submit rates jumped from ~8% to ~31%, and success rates from ~5% to ~21%. The drop in volume is real, but it's largely noise removal. The transfer flow itself is working better for users who truly need it.

</Callout>

<div class="report-quote-grid">
<Callout title="P01 on the search bar:">

"I don't understand the difference between 'Search and transfer a domain' to the search and transfer button. When I click to start typing it shows more options that I was expecting, it gives options that I didn't know that existed — this can be confusing."

</Callout>
<Callout title="P02 on bulk transfer error handling:">

"I copy/pasted the three domains in the input field because I thought the 'transfer' option would be efficient enough to identify them — like how email fields handle multiple addresses. I was expecting the same happened here."

</Callout>
<Callout title="P02 on transfer confidence:">

"I never done a transfer before. I don't know if the process will put my website offline, how much time and steps it will take. I wish it had here some bullet points with that information to help me feel more confident."

</Callout>
<Callout title="P03 on workflow consistency:">

"The transfer process is so fast that I'm afraid I'm doing something wrong. I would like to have some information to reassure that everything is ok — that I won't be charged twice, for example."

</Callout>

<ReportSection number="03" title="Cross-Study Connections" description="Where qualitative signals and quantitative data reinforce each other.">

<Annotation number="1" title="The April change was directionally right, but users still struggle once they find the widget." text="The 21% success rate post-change and the qualitative issues around error handling and format validation suggest that the funnel is intentional now, but the experience inside the widget has friction that costs conversions." />

<Annotation number="2" title="Mobile is a real gap." text="Post-April, mobile drops from 23% to 9% of widget interactions — but domain transfers on mobile are a legitimate use case. The usability data shows that the bulk transfer flow was already hard enough on desktop. On mobile, it's likely worse." />

<Annotation number="3" title="Discoverability is the upstream problem." text="Users in the usability test repeatedly couldn't find the transfer entry point. The quantitative data confirms this — most users who interact with the widget submit (92%+ drop off before submitting). If users can't find or trust the entry point, the funnel never starts." />

<Annotation number="4" title="Trust and clarity are conversion levers." text="Multiple participants expressed anxiety about what happens during a transfer (downtime, double billing, timelines). This isn't a cosmetic UX issue — it likely suppresses transfers that would otherwise complete. Reassurance copy and process transparency are low-effort, high-impact fixes." />

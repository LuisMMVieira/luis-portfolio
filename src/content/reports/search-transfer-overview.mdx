<ReportSection number="01" title="Bulk Transfer Widget — Analytics" description="BI-13028 · Jan 1 – Aug 31, 2024. On April 11, the widget label changed from 'Transfer' to 'Bulk Transfer' and moved behind an extra click.">

<Callout title="April 11 change:">

Daily interactions dropped by **~95%** — from ~1,004 to ~47 per day. This is not a failure: it filters out users who were accidentally landing on a bulk flow they didn't need.

<ReportSection number="05" title="Cross-Study Connections" description="Where analytics and qualitative data reinforce each other across all 3 studies.">

<Annotation number="1" title="The April change was directionally right — Round 2 confirms the redesign pushed further." text="Analytics: 95% drop in volume, 4× increase in success rate. Round 1 showed why — accidental users flooded the widget. Round 2 shows the redesigned modal works: search confusion gone, task completion up from 44% clean to 75% clean." />

<Annotation number="2" title="Transfer discoverability is the persistent upstream problem." text="Round 1: users couldn't find the transfer entry point. Analytics: 92%+ drop-off before submitting. Round 2: 3 of 4 users still went to the menu first. The search bar works once found — but users don't expect transfers to live there. This needs a secondary entry point." />

<Annotation number="3" title="Mobile remains an unresolved gap." text="Post-April, mobile dropped from 23% to 9% of widget interactions. Neither usability round tested mobile. Domain transfers on mobile are a legitimate use case — and likely worse than desktop given the bulk transfer complexity." />

<Annotation number="4" title="Trust and micro-interactions are the next frontier." text="Round 1 surfaced anxiety about downtime, double billing, timelines. Round 2 surfaced invisible EPP tooltips and confusing mid-flow copy. The big structural problems are solved — what remains is polish: reassurance copy, inline guidance, consistent iconography." />
